Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an iconic white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who passed away during childbirth in the 17th century. Known for its breathtaking beauty and intricate architectural details, the Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most recognized symbols of India. Read more

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a magnificent historical monument located in Delhi, India. It stands as a symbol of the rich architectural and cultural heritage of the country. Built during the early 13th century, the Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the tallest brick minarets in the world, reaching a height of 72.5 meters (237.8 feet). Read more

India Gate

India Gate

India Gate is a prominent war memorial located in the heart of New Delhi, India. It stands as a powerful symbol of honor and remembrance for the soldiers of the Indian Army who sacrificed their lives during World War I and subsequent conflicts. The memorial was originally known as the All India War Memorial. Read more

Golden Temple

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple is in fact a small part of a much bigger gurdwara complex, known as the Harmandir Sahib. Guru Ram Das acquired the land for the gurdwara in the 16th century. His successor, Guru Arjan began construction, building it lower than the rest of the city in a gesture of humility.
